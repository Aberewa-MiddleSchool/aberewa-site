Aberewa Middle School

Our Vision

Aberewa is for 7th and 8th graders with intellectual and mental exceptionalities. We reject one-size-fits-all education. Our model is rooted in Akan culture, restorative justice, and no high-stakes testing.

  • Specialized Support: Designed for students with intellectual and mental exceptionalities
  • Anti-Standardization: We reject one-size-fits-all approaches and rigid testing structures
  • Cultural Grounding: We integrate the philosophy and values of Akan culture
  • Justice-Centered: Restorative justice replaces punitive discipline models
  • No High-Stakes Testing: We evaluate learning in ways that reflect growth, not pressure

Curriculum

Academic Track

  • English Language Arts (ELA): Reading and writing strategies grounded in identity, storytelling, and voice
  • Mathematics: Functional math skills with real-world application and problem-solving
  • History: Local, diasporic, and African history rooted in resistance and empowerment
  • Geoscience: Earth systems, environmental justice, and climate impact on marginalized communities
  • Interstellar Science: Space, stars, and speculative Black futures rooted in science and creativity

Life Skills Track

  • Cooking: Meals from African and diasporic cultures, basic nutrition, and food independence
  • Hygiene: Cleanliness, grooming, and self-care rooted in dignity
  • Technology: Basic digital literacy, typing, and media creation
  • Mobility: Navigating communities, use of public transit, and spatial awareness
  • Personal Finance: Budgeting, saving, understanding money, and empowerment through economics

All Students

  • Afro-Manga Art: Drawing, storytelling, and visual culture inspired by both anime and African traditions
  • Akan Language: Basic Twi speaking, proverbs, and cultural literacy
  • Storytelling: Oral and written expression, myth-building, and liberation pedagogy
