Grades 7–8 · Complete framework

Curriculum built for dignity, access, culture, and growth.

Aberewa Middle School rejects one-size-fits-all education. Students receive rigorous, accessible learning grounded in Akan wisdom, the African diaspora, New Orleans, restorative practice, creativity, and meaningful preparation for life.

Our instructional commitments

Individualized access

Teachers use IEP goals, accommodations, assistive technology, explicit instruction, and responsive pacing.

Purposeful repetition

Students revisit important knowledge through discussion, music, visuals, hands-on work, practice, and real applications.

Multiple demonstrations

Students may explain learning through writing, speech, performance, models, art, technology, and community projects.

Akan-rooted community

Learning emphasizes wisdom, responsibility, relationships, memory, and the contributions of African and diasporic peoples.

Restorative practice

Dialogue, reflection, repair, belonging, and growth guide classroom culture and conflict response.

No high-stakes testing

Progress is demonstrated through portfolios, projects, conferences, performances, and standards-aligned teacher evidence.
