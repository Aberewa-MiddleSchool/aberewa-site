Areas where support can make a difference

Planning and organizational development

Legal, accounting, governance, strategic planning, feasibility research, grant development, and regulatory preparation.

Curriculum and educational technology

Accessible curriculum development, assistive technology, educational AI music, learning materials, and multiple ways for students to demonstrate understanding.

Neurodivergent student supports

Sensory-friendly resources, individualized learning supports, communication tools, related services, restorative practices, and family engagement.

Educator development

Teacher recruitment, special-education expertise, professional learning, culturally responsive instruction, and staff preparation.

Facilities and accessibility

Site research, architectural and accessibility planning, acquisition or renovation studies, safety, transportation, and a long-term campus vision.

Research and community partnerships

Collaborative research, program evaluation, university partnerships, community listening, and evidence-building around Akan-rooted inclusive education.

Current organizational profile

Organization

Aberewa Middle School

Louisiana charter number: 46506074N

Education classification: Elementary and Secondary Schools (NAICS 611110)

Educational focus

A proposed Akan-rooted middle-school program designed to support neurodivergent students through individualized access, cultural connection, restorative practice, and rigorous interdisciplinary learning.

Current status disclosure

Aberewa Middle School does not currently have federal 501(c)(3) tax-exempt recognition. The school is exploring appropriate nonprofit development and fiscal-sponsorship pathways. Grant eligibility will depend on each funder's rules and the legal structure required for a particular award.

Partnership opportunities

Potential partners may contribute professional expertise, technical assistance, in-kind resources, research collaboration, educator preparation, facility planning, sponsorship, or funding. A conversation does not create a financial commitment.
